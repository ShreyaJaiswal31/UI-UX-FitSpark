# FitSpark - Mobile App Design (Human Computer Interaction)

FitSpark is a mobile fitness application that motivates users to stay active by integrating wearable technology, daily challenges, and social competition. It tracks user activity such as steps, heart rate, and calories burned. FitSpark caters to both busy individuals looking for short, engaging workouts and fitness enthusiasts seeking detailed tracking and competition.
The purpose of this user testing was to evaluate the usability of the FitSpark prototype. The main goal was to gather real user feedback to identify pain points, understand user behavior, and inform improvements to the prototype.

## üß≠ Scenarios

### **Scenario 1: Sarah Johnson - Office Quick Workout**

- **Context:** Has 20 minutes at work for a quick workout  
- **Goal:** Complete a short, guided workout  
- **Steps:**
  - Opens FitSpark on iPhone  
  - Chooses "Quick Workouts" > 15-min meditation  
  - Follows instructions, tracks heart rate via Apple Watch  
  - Earns 50 points and unlocks a badge  

**Outcome:**  
Sarah feels refreshed and meets part of her weekly goal.

---

### **Scenario 2: Mike Thompson - Gym Challenge**

- **Context:** Sunday morning at the gym  
- **Goal:** Log a weightlifting session and challenge friends  
- **Steps:**
  - Opens FitSpark on Android  
  - Selects "Weightlifting"  
  - Logs sets, reps, and weights  
  - Shares summary on social feed  
  - Challenges friends to beat his record  

**Outcome:**  
Mike logs his workout, motivates friends, and pushes harder next time.
